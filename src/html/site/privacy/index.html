Privacy Policy


In order to sign up and use our service you’ll need to provide limited personal information. We appreciate the trust you place in True to be good stewards of this information. We only use this information to provide access to the service and to improve the user experience. We never sell or monetize user data.


We have written this privacy policy to comply with the most advanced privacy law in the United States, the California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA), and we’re applying the same CCPA benefits to all our other users as well.


Information We Get When You Use Our Services

When you use our services, we collect information about which of those services you’ve used and how you’ve used them.


Here’s a fuller explanation of the types of information we collect when you use our services:


  • Usage Information. We collect information about your activity through our services. For example, we may collect information about:
    • how you interact with our services
    • how you communicate with other True users, such as their names, the time and date of your communications, the number of messages you exchange with your friends, which friends you exchange messages with the most, and your interactions with messages (such as when you open a message or capture a screenshot).
  • Content Information. We collect content you create on our services and information about the content you create or provide, such as if the recipient has viewed the content and the metadata that is provided with the content.
  • Device Information. We collect information from and about the devices you use. For example, we collect:
    • information about your hardware and software, such as the hardware model, operating system version, device memory, advertising identifiers, unique application identifiers, apps installed, unique device identifiers, browser type, keyboards installed, language, battery level, and time zone;
    • information from device sensors, such as accelerometers, gyroscopes, compasses, microphones, and whether you have headphones connected; and
    • information about your wireless and mobile network connections, such as mobile phone number, service provider, IP address, and signal strength.
  • Device Phonebook and Contact List. Because our services are all about communicating with friends, we may — with your permission — collect information from your device’s phonebook and contact list.
  • Camera and Photos. Many of our services require us to collect images and other information from your device’s camera and photos. For example, you won’t be able to upload photos from your camera roll unless we can access your camera or photos.
  • Information Collected by Cookies and Other Technologies. Like most online services and mobile applications, we may use cookies and other technologies, such as web beacons, web storage, and unique advertising identifiers, to collect information about your activity, browser, and device. We may also use these technologies to collect information when you interact with services we offer through one of our partners, such as advertising and commerce features.
  • Log Information. We also collect log information when you use our website, such as:
    • details about how you’ve used our services;
    • device ID and information, such as your web browser type and language;
    • access times;
    • pages viewed;
    • IP address;
    • identifiers associated with cookies or other technologies that may uniquely identify your device or browser; and
    • pages you visited before or after navigating to our website.
    • If you or another user uploads their contact list, we may combine information from that user’s contact list with other information we have collected about you so you can connect as friends

Our information practices regarding non-personal information

  • Technical data. In order to maintain the integrity of our systems and to monitor and improve their performance we collect technical information typically collected by websites and apps: device characteristics (including device ID for mobile devices), operating system, browser type, dates and times logged in, use statistics, metadata for images, etc.
  • Aggregate and anonymized data. To better understand our users we may use de-identified/anonymized data and aggregate data through sources such as an IDFA and an AAID to enable us to gauge what parts of our products are popular and how they are used generally.
  • Contact Lists. To enable you to access your contacts from within the True app, you can sync your contact list to True. Once your contacts are synced, you'll receive notifications when anyone on your list joins True. We’ll never use your contact list for anything other than to provide a better user experience (we won’t contact them or sell or share the information on your contact list).

Our information practices regarding personal information

  • Recent information practices: During the most recent 12 months we have collected the following categories of personal information about some of our members: name, phone number, email address, and IP address. We have not disclosed, sold, or shared any categories of personal information about our members to any third parties.
  • Current information practices:
    • Personal information:
      • We continue to collect the personal information described above in 1.
      • We collect personal information about our members only from our members, and not from any other categories of sources.
      • We do not share, sell, or disclose personal information about our members to anyone.
      • We collect personal information to enable our product and network to function.
    • Sensitive personal information: We don’t collect such information.
    • Information you publicly disclose: Whatever information you make available to the public by disclosing it to other users – by designating it as “viewable” or by posting it to your profile or in comments – is not personal information. We do not analyze, data-mine, sell, or otherwise use the information you post as part of your “profile information.”
  • Your rights regarding information we collect. You have the right to:
    • obtain from us the specific pieces of personal information we have collected about you.
    • opt-out of the sale or sharing of your personal information.
    • correct inaccurate personal information we have about you.
    • delete the personal information we have about you.
  • If you exercise these rights there won’t be any adverse consequences from us.

  • TO CORRECT INACCURATE PERSONAL INFORMATION OR TO DELETE YOUR PERSONAL INFORMATION: Simply log into your account and make the corrections / deletions you desire.
  • TO REQUEST DETAILS ABOUT OUR PERSONAL INFORMATION PRACTICES AS THEY AFFECT YOU, CLICK HERE: CCPA@trytrue.com. SUCH DETAILS INCLUDE:
    • COLLECTION: The categories of personal information as well as the specific pieces of personal information we have collected about you, and the categories of sources from which that information has been collected.
    • DISCLOSURE: The categories of personal information about you that we have disclosed, sold or shared, the business or commercial purposes for which we have collected, disclosed, sold and/or shared it; the categories of third parties to whom we have disclosed personal information, and to whom we sold (or with whom we shared) your personal information; and the categories of personal information about you that we have disclosed for a business purpose and the categories of persons to whom it was disclosed.
  • TO LIMIT THE USE OF YOUR SENSITIVE PERSONAL INFORMATION, CLICK HERE: CCPA@trytrue.com (We don’t collect or use sensitive personal information, but we’re required to include this function.)  If you need assistance in exercising your rights or don’t wish to use the “click here” buttons, you may email us at CCPA@trytrue.com and we will assist you.

If you use our website: browsers have a number of privacy-related settings and extensions, such as allowing or blocking various types of cookies, allowing or preventing videos from automatically running, and a “do not track” setting (more info: https://www.eff.org/issues/do-not-track). Our website does not respond to “do not track” browser settings because there’s nothing to respond to: we don’t track your activities across websites.
